The Good Liar - Revenge is best served late and elaborate

The same way we should not judge a book by it’s cover, we should probably not judge a book by it’s cover. But once I saw The Good Liar’s poster with the “Read between the lies” pun, I was interested - I am a sucker for puns.

The poster is in fact quite interesting to look at after watching the movie.

But first, the basics. I enjoyed the movie, I found it to be extremely entertaining, as it kept my mind racing, trying to guess what was coming next. Nonetheless, I was left disappointed.

The first 45 minutes or so seem to steer you in a certain direction but then after that the movie falls a bit flat, in my opinion.

The blame is not on the actors, for both Helen Mirren and Iam McKellen are superb in their roles. The way they act, with all their intricacies and well developed characters, make this movie worth the watch.

Mirren’s Betty is charmingly lovely and McKellen’s Roy always leaves you wondering. The way both characters change expressions and tones as soon as one of the other leaves the scene, always keep you on your toes.

The problem is, I think, in the script. It is a book adaptation and, as such, maybe something was left out. I am sorry to report I have not read the book, so only readers will have that insight. In my eyes, from a certain point on, the plot seems rushed - which I find surprising, since according to Imdb, the novel’s author was somehow involved in the script as well.

As I said, the poster is a good indicator of what you might expect. The poster seems to mimic the colors of the ying and yang and in a sense that is what you can expect: two opposite forces battling each other, each trying to reach a certain goal.

The concept of karmic retribution is well presented in this movie and without spoiling it too much what I can say is that the line between justice and revenge is a thin one, and you will be left reflecting on it long after the movie is over.
